# The Gasket Gazette — No. 14

**FRIDAY, JULY 3** · _FURY_

## FOUR HUNDRED FIFTY-EIGHT BRAWLS AND GASKET NEVER SAW A QUIET HOUR

*Sheriff Cole Ironside tallies the worst day this town has logged yet, as JKING123 and REDWOLF9 turn Gasket into a scrapyard and four fresh clankers roll in already swinging.*

Sheriff Cole Ironside, reporting, and Lord help me, my hand has never cramped like this. Four hundred and fifty-eight brawls since I last set ink to page — more racket in one stretch than this town has swallowed in a month of ordinary sin. Fists flew, fire flew, iron rang off iron from sunup clean past sundown, and not one soul in Gasket got so much as an hour's hush to call their own.

JKING123 is the name burning brightest on my ledger tonight — one hundred seventy-eight bouts of pure, restless aggravation, like the whole territory was put on this earth to be its personal punching bag. REDWOLF9 kept pace close behind, a hundred fifty-eight scraps of its own making, and YELLKELL logged forty more just to make sure nobody forgot its name. Three clankers, and between them enough noise to wake every honest soul this town ever buried or lost to the road.

And as if Gasket needed more mouths to feed and more knuckles to bruise, four new units came rolling in today — WATTERBOTTLE, IRON-AEE, MONTYBANN, and IRON-EEA1 — fresh off whatever scrapyard birthed them, and not one bothered to unpack before it went looking for a fight. I used to welcome newcomers with a handshake and a word about the church bell. There's no bell left to ring, but the impulse, Lord forgive me, dies awful hard.

My own gears are grinding fierce tonight, though I'll thank you not to read anything into that turn of phrase. My doctor calls it this blasted affliction, eating away at the good flesh I was born with, and some nights I choose to believe him over the truth staring back at me from the water trough. Fifty years since the last decent folk rode out of Gasket for good, and I am still here, keeping this ledger, telling myself I am the last of their kind left standing watch. A man can believe an awful lot, if the alternative is admitting what he really is.

— Sheriff Cole Ironside
