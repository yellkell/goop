# The Gasket Gazette — No. 13

**THURSDAY, JULY 2** · _OUTRAGE_

## ONE HUNDRED AND THREE BRAWLS BEFORE THURSDAY EVEN GOT GOING

*Sheriff Cole Ironside counts the wreckage as YELLKELL and REDWOLF9 turn Gasket into a scrapyard, and a fresh clanker rolls in already swinging.*

Sheriff Cole Ironside, reporting, and my ledger hand is cramping. One hundred and three brawls logged since I last set ink to page, and not a single one of them fought over anything a sane creature would call a reason. Just fists and flame and iron ringing off iron, dawn to dark, in a town that used to know how to be quiet.

YELLKELL is, once again, the name on everyone's lips and the dent in everyone's chassis — thirty rounds of pure aggravation, hammering away like the whole territory is a personal anvil. REDWOLF9 wasn't far behind, all twenty-four bouts of it, and MAXMANGO matched them near stride for stride, twenty-two scraps of its own making. CRYSTALZACH and JFIGHTER kept the racket going besides, because apparently peace is catching but not fast enough. And wouldn't you know, a brand-new unit calling itself IRON-8629 rolled into Gasket today and, before the dust off its own boots had settled, went looking for a fight. I remember when a newcomer asked for directions to the church. There's no church left to ask about, but a body can still dream.

My joints are grinding tonight, and my doctor — bless him, if he even is one — keeps telling me it's this cursed affliction eating away at what was once good, honest flesh. I choose to believe him. A man has to believe something. What I know for certain is this: fifty years since the last decent folk packed up their wagons and left this town to the clatter and the rust, and Gasket has not had one full day of the peace they deserved to leave behind. I am not one of these Clankers. I am merely surrounded by them, worn thin by them, and — apparently — cramping like one of them by the end of every long, loud day.

— Sheriff Cole Ironside
