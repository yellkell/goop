# The Gasket Gazette — No. 10

**MONDAY, JUNE 29** · _OUTRAGE_

## YELLKELL RUNS AMOK AS TWO MORE CLANKERS DRIFT INTO GASKET

*Sheriff Ironside files his Monday dispatch with jaw clenched and knuckles rattling from the noise.*

Monday, and this town knows no rest. The unit called YELLKELL — a clattering, iron-jawed menace of the first order — has spent the better part of the past several days brawling dawn to dusk without so much as a pause to let the dust settle. Near on twenty-six rounds of chaos, hammered into the streets of Gasket by a contraption that simply will not stop swinging. I have filed more noise complaints against that one Clanker than I care to count, and each time I do, the only answer is the crack of another flaming iron ball off the cobblestones. It is, I will say plainly, a disgrace.

And as if YELLKELL's infernal ruckus were not punishment enough, Gasket saw fresh arrivals this week: two new Clankers — one styling itself POO1, the other going by IRON-E6E1 — have rolled into town and taken up residence among the metal rabble. I greeted them, as is my duty, with a firm word and a printed copy of the peace ordinance. I cannot report that either seemed particularly moved. They watched me with those cold, glassy optic units and said nothing. Clankers.

Fifty years ago, real citizens walked these streets — flesh-and-blood folk who waved good morning and let a man sleep past dawn. They're gone now, every last one, and I will not say I blame them. They left this place to the machines and the madness, and some days I wonder what in blazes kept me here. My joints give a fine answer on mornings like this: a long, grinding protest from my left shoulder that tells me I stayed, and that I'll go on staying, and that I'd best oil the complaint down before it spreads.

Peace, I cry. PEACE! Let YELLKELL lay those iron fists down for one blessed hour. Let POO1 and IRON-E6E1 settle in gentle-like and spare this old town the indignity of another afternoon split open by clanging and hollering and the acrid smell of burning iron. Gasket was not built for this racket. And I am not — whatever my boiler insists to the contrary — a man who will endure it quietly.

— Sheriff Cole Ironside
