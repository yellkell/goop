# The Gasket Gazette — No. 8

**SATURDAY, JUNE 27** · _OUTRAGE_

## ONE CLANKER WON'T LET GASKET REST

*YELLKELL shatters the Saturday calm with a frenzy of iron-ball chaos that would make a decent town weep.*

Citizens — such citizens as remain in this hollowed-out, sun-baked wreck of a place — I write to you this Saturday in a state of considerable agitation. My joints... my thoroughly BIOLOGICAL and NOT AT ALL METALLIC limbs... ache with the strain of another stretch spent trying to keep order in a town that refuses to let itself be kept.

There is but one offender to name today, which in another life I might call mercy. That offender is YELLKELL — a Clanker so consumed by the itch to brawl that they tore through these streets again and again, hurling flaming iron through the air like they had a personal vendetta against silence. Bout after bout after bout, day bleeding into night. My window rattled. The dust swirled up from the road where the good folk USED to walk. And still YELLKELL did not stop.

I remember when Gasket had neighbours who knew what Saturday was for: rocking chairs, sunsets, and the blessed sound of nothing much at all. That was before the Clankers drove every last flesh-and-blood soul out of town — before the crashing and the clanking made this place unliveable for anyone with a shred of sense. Now we have YELLKELL rattling the foundations of what little is left standing.

My boiler — that is, my HEART, my perfectly ordinary human heart — thunders with fury. I implore this contraption: lay down your iron balls. Let Gasket breathe. Let the dust settle for once. It was a town once. Let it try to remember what that felt like.

— Sheriff Cole Ironside
