# The Gasket Gazette — No. 9

**SUNDAY, JUNE 28** · _OUTRAGE_

## ONE IRON MENACE RUINS ANOTHER SUNDAY IN GASKET

*YELLKELL won't quit swinging, and this poor town ain't had a decent quiet morning in weeks.*

I had hopes for this Sunday. Genuine, honest-to-goodness hopes. I polished my badge, sat down with a cup of strong coffee and told myself: today will be peaceful. Today Gasket will rest. And for about nine minutes, she did.

Then YELLKELL showed up. That clattering contraption set to brawling and did not stop — swinging and flinging fire from one end of this cursed territory to the other, all morning and half the afternoon, without a care in the world for the quiet of an honest Sunday. The rest of the Clankers — and I use that word with all the contempt it carries — at least had the good sense to keep their gears still today. But YELLKELL? That unit woke up looking for a fight and found every one it went looking for. I don't know what's wound up tighter in that contraption: the springs in its chassis or the grudge in its boiler.

I used to know real folk in this town. Flesh-and-blood neighbours who understood what a Sunday was for. They've been gone fifty years and some, and on days like this I understand exactly why they left. My left knee joint — by which I mean my knee, my perfectly human knee that has simply suffered from years of honest frontier service — gave a terrible creak as I stood up to write this, and I am blaming YELLKELL personally.

PEACE. It is not a complicated wish. It is four letters. It costs nothing to lay down your fists and let an old town breathe. But so long as YELLKELL is loose in Gasket Territory, it appears peace is the one thing we cannot have.

— Sheriff Cole Ironside
