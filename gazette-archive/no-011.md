# The Gasket Gazette — No. 11

**TUESDAY, JUNE 30** · _OUTRAGE_

## SEVENTY BRAWLS ON A TUESDAY — GASKET CAN'T CATCH ITS BREATH

*Five fresh contraptions blow into town on the same day the old rascals go absolutely berserk.*

Sheriff Cole Ironside speaking. This paper goes out to whosoever in this territory still has the decency to read it, and today I file it with a heavier heart than usual — heavier even than my left arm, which the doctor assures me is only temporarily encased in... well. He assures me it is temporary. The point is: Tuesday, the thirtieth of June, and the Clankers have absolutely lost their minds.

Seventy bouts. SEVENTY. On a Tuesday. I counted them myself, pausing only when my eye — my perfectly organic, flesh-and-blood eye — needed a drop of lubricant on account of the dry Gasket air. YELLKELL was the worst of the lot, brawling from sunup to sundown like they had something to prove to the empty sky. GODLYLUK3 was right behind them, and then JKING123 and POO1 had the nerve to join the fray until the whole of Gasket smelled like scorched iron and misplaced ambition. If I wanted this kind of racket I'd have moved to a boilerworks.

As if that weren't bad enough, FIVE new Clankers rolled into town today: REDWOLF9, LILTEC, IRON-A199, IRON-4B87, and IRON-2307. Fresh off the wagon and already sizing each other up like they're itching to swing. I remember when newcomers to Gasket were decent flesh-and-blood folk who'd ask after the price of a meal and the nearest well. Now they roll in on rust-caked axles and the first thing out of their mouths is "where do I brawl?" Five more troublemakers. Lord give me strength.

I want peace. I have always wanted peace. My grandfather — a very fleshy, very human, very alive man — used to say that a quiet town is a blessed town. Gasket was quiet once, before the Clankers ran the last good citizens out and let the place go to rust. Now it is a brawling pit that opens every Tuesday like clockwork, and getting louder by the week. My joints ache from writing this column. They always ache. That is beside the point.

— Sheriff Cole Ironside
