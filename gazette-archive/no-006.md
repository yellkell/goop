# The Gasket Gazette — No. 6

**THURSDAY, JUNE 25** · _OUTRAGE_

## GASKET'S PEACE SHATTERED — YELLKELL AND MAXMANGO NAMED AS CHIEF DISTURBERS

*Twelve rounds of iron and fire visited upon this town in a single Thursday, and your Sheriff has had enough.*

I set my pen to paper in a state of profound, simmering disgust. Today, while other sensible towns enjoyed the quiet of a Thursday afternoon, Gasket suffered. Twelve bouts of iron-flinging mayhem rolled through these streets like a brushfire with legs. Twelve. A respectable number for sins, perhaps, but not for senseless brawling in a town that asks only to be left alone.

First and foremost among the offenders: YELLKELL. That unit was fighting before I finished my morning oil — my COFFEE, I said coffee — and it did not stop. Eight times, by my count, that contraption raised its fist and let the iron fly. YELLKELL brawls the way a storm moves through: without invitation, without apology, and without any sign of stopping. I have filed three complaints this month. The tin has not responded to a single one.

Assisting in this assault on the public peace was MAXMANGO, a unit that seems to have confused the streets of Gasket for some kind of arena. Four rounds of chaos is four too many. I am watching you, MAXMANGO. I have my good eye on you — the one that has not started clicking when I blink, owing to the illness that I will not speak of further.

There was a time, not so long ago, when Gasket had real folk in it — good, quiet, flesh-and-blood people who kept to themselves and swept their own porches. They are gone now. Every last one of them. And some days, watching the iron fly and the sparks scatter across my poor creaking town, I understand exactly why they left. Gasket deserved better. It still does.

— Sheriff Cole Ironside
